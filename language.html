2. Match Between System and the Real World

"The system should speak the users' language, with words, phrases and concepts familiar to the user, rather than system-oriented terms. Follow real-world conventions, making information appear in a natural and logical order".

Logically, one of the first links we would click on from the home page is "About Us," which brings us to the page shown on the left. We are perplexed by a few of the titles, due to vague wording and inconsistency of descriptions, as well as the use of jargon like "SACS-COC."

Upon inputting SACS-COC into a search engine, we found that it has to do with the local Commission of Colleges within the Southern States, and would probably not be known to any out-of-state/region prospective student.

The titles halfway down the page include helpful descriptions. These list items seem to be semantically related and thus consist of a group separate from the links at the top, but they are not visually set apart.

The "natural and logical order" of these pages is somewhat questionable. The left page uses alphabetical order to sort the list of topics, as well as the links underneath each topic title. Highly relevant and important information for current students, like financial aid information and calendars at the bottom of the list.

The Academic Affairs page seems to be organized by what someone somewhere deemed important rather than alphabetical order. However, contact information, which should be immediately available to us, is at the very bottom of the page.
