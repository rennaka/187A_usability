7. Flexibility and Efficiency of Use

"Accelerators -- unseen by the novice user -- may often speed up the interaction for the expert user such that the system can cater to both inexperienced and experienced users. Allow users to tailor frequent actions".

Forms are complex things and mostly ill-conceived. Here for instance, users have to delete the title of the input field before they enter their own information. We tried to enter our information without deleting these titles, and we submitted: No error Message! This is an example of inefficiency of use.

For instance, on this page registrar is just a small unit of text. It is difficult to know where to start when confronted with a page like this. There is too much information.

According to Nielsen, "Every extra unit of information in a dialogue competes with the relevant units of information and diminishes their relative visibility."
