8. Aesthetic and Minimal Design

"Dialogues should not contain information which is irrelevant or rarely needed. Every extra unit of information in a dialogue competes with the relevant units of information and diminishes their relative visibility".

The way that this information is presented is a little intimidating to read through.

We would have to say that this approach does not satisfy a minimalist style when presenting this information. When presenting information to the user, it should be clear where the location of the most relevant data should be.

When it comes to aesthetics and minimal design, this email instruction page has to be the exact opposite of what should be expected from a website. These big blocks of unnecessary text discourage us from using this tool.

In contemporary web design, this type of explanation of sending an email should hopefully never happen. The text could be eliminated with better design of the form and form validation, which could make some of this instruction built-in. For example, there could be two separate boxes for the last name and the first name.
