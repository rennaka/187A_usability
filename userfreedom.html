3. User Control and Freedom

"Users often choose system functions by mistake and will need a clearly marked "emergency exit" to leave the unwanted state without having to go through an extended dialogue. Support undo and redo."

Usually, in order to facilitate the navigation of users website designers create a home page button on each page. This button is called "emergency exit", and it is a simple way to let users feel in control of your site.

Many of the links on this website take us to external sites, like the one shown to the left. Very few of these external sites use the same template as the university home site, and many do not include a link back to the university home site.

This gives us the feeling of being jostled around a collection of incoherent sites, and we have to rely on browser functions to give us control over our navigation.

Clicking on the image on the home page brings us to a page called "Photo Collection" in the "About Us" section. The URL and title both clearly indicate that there are more pictures to be seen, but there is no way for us to access any other photo, other than returning to the home page and reloading it--a ridiculous workaround to an easily solvable usability issue. Adding a simple thumbnail gallery on this page would be a great way to give us more control.
