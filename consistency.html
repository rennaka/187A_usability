4. Consistency and Standards

"Users should not have to wonder whether different words, situations, or actions mean the same thing. Follow platform conventions".

Upon looking at these two layouts, we noticed that they were quite different. With such a drastic difference in layout and color, we decided that this may not be the best practice.

Perhaps the designers should have choosen a layout that was more consistent so that users will not have to learn new layouts.

This page is an interesting example of consistency because this layout is the same for each section (Prospective student, current students, Almuni... see image on the left). As a result, you can easily navigate in the website.

However, the redundancy is questionable. To avoid this problem, one solution may be to italicize the label in the sub navigation on the right. This will show the user that they are on this proper page.

This site has a variety of link styles, which causes confusion if we do not pay close attention. The examples shown to the left are in pairs that have the same structure but different styles.

The pair on the top right have a title in red and a a couple phrases of desription in black. The entire shaded region is clickable on the top link from the "Alumni, Donors & Friends" page. The example below it follows convention instead, with an underline to indicate that the title is the link portion.

The difference between the pair on the bottom left caused us the most confusion. The one on top has a black title with individual links below it, separated by inline bullet points. The one below it uses a slightly smaller title, and although the text below it is also divided by inline bullet points, it is all one link. This inconsistency produces an unexpected result when we try to click on one of the topics under the "Graduate School" title.

The other pair of links is a combination of the other two groups. The title is red, and the description below it is an inline list in black. Such a wide variety of appearances, rather than signalling to us the type of content, makes the site seem chaotic and unpredictable.
